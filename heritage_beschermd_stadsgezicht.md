# Beschermd stadsgezicht — Amstelveen rules (reference)

_Saved 30 May 2026. Applies to Catharina van Clevepark 40 (Randwijck) and any other Amstelveen property in a protected townscape._

## What it is

Catharina van Clevepark 40 sits in a **gemeentelijk (municipal) beschermd stadsgezicht** — the 1949–51 complex was designated by the gemeente, not by the Rijk. This is the **lighter** category. Crucially, municipal designations keep the normal *vergunningvrij bouwen* (permit-free building) rules in force; nationally designated gezichten do not. Amstelveen has roughly 12–20 such municipal designations.

The designation protects the **character of the area** (streetscape, ensemble, main structure) — not the individual building as a monument. The building is **not** a monument: no maintenance obligations, no inspections, no subsidy, no extra tax.

## What is protected

Only the **facades and roof planes facing the public space** (the street side). Explicitly:

- The **interior is not protected** — free to modernise.
- Parts **not visible from the public road** (rear) follow ordinary building rules.

## Permit-free (no omgevingsvergunning)

- Interior modernisation — kitchen, bathroom, layout, finishes.
- On the **rear / non-street-visible** side: solar panels on the rear roof plane, a dakkapel, roof windows, a ground-floor rear extension, a shed — all permit-free if they meet normal vergunningvrij criteria.
- **Regular maintenance that doesn't change the appearance**: repaint in the same colour, replace roof tiles with the same type, repointing, wood-rot repair.

## Permit required (assessed by the independent Erfgoedcommissie)

Anything that changes the **street-visible** appearance:

- Changes to the street-facing facade or roof.
- **Solar panels on a roof plane visible from the public road** — specifically no longer permit-free here.
- **Plastic window frames (kunststof kozijnen)** in street-facing facades/roof — specifically called out.
- Visible extensions, front dormers, fences (erfafscheidingen), works in the public space.

These are not automatic refusals — they're judged case-by-case against the welstandsnota / gebiedsbrochure criteria, with the committee favouring original style, materials, and the characteristic streetscape.

## Practical takeaways for this property

- **Interior renovation is unconstrained** — which fits, since the flat is "nog naar eigen smaak te moderniseren."
- **No front-roof solar panels** without a permit; if ever wanted, they'd go on the rear roof (permit-free there).
- **Already label A**, so the energy-upgrade friction is largely moot for now.
- **Due-diligence flag:** the listing already mentions **kunststof kozijnen**. Plastic frames on the street side now require a permit, so confirm with the makelaar/gemeente that the existing frames were placed *with* permission (or predate the designation). Otherwise it's a small inherited risk.

## Bestemmingsplan / Omgevingswet

A "waarde – cultuurhistorie" dubbelbestemming may apply (largely symbolic; building mass already conserving-fixed). Since the Omgevingswet took effect 1 Jan 2024, these rules live in the municipal **omgevingsplan** — verify the exact do's and don'ts for the specific block via the Omgevingsloket or Amstelveen's heritage adviser (afdeling Stedelijke Ontwikkeling, Cultureel Erfgoed).
