## Risks found (ranked)

1. **Forward VvE capital programme — MEDIUM (the main watch-item).** The 09-04-2026 ALV committed two big jobs from reserve: **chimneys €31,656.63** (Dakexpert Holland — full re-pointing + new lead) and **painting €40,435** (JMG — 27 berging doors, boeidelen, 9 portieken). Together €72.1k ≈ 98% of the 1-1-2025 reserve — **but** two years of €33,275 dotatie flow in over 2025–26, so the fund replenishes to ~€67k before other spend, and the minutes confirm the works are covered **with no suppletie** (Q16e "nee"). The real unknown is next: **cast-iron soil stacks (standleidingen), ~30 yrs old, flagged for full replacement** (one portiek already defective), plus steel kozijnen (~5 units) budgeted 2028 and a **new MJOP** to be drawn up — all of which point to a likely **rise in the monthly contribution** and a possible future levy. Budget a €5–15k capex contingency for nr 81's share over 5–10 yrs and expect the €156/mo to climb.
2. **Freehold not yet *documented* — MEDIUM (paperwork, not defect).** The "kadaster"-named file in the set is actually the **energy-label** PDF; there is **no Kadaster eigendomsinformatie or leveringsakte** on file. Eigen grond is strongly *indicated* (canon €0) but unproven. **Request the Kadaster extract + splitsingsakte/leveringsakte**, confirm eigen grond, and check any erfdienstbaarheid over the rear path (achterpad) that serves the bergingen. Make it a koopakte voorbehoud.
3. **1955 building, several "niet bekend" disclosures — MEDIUM.** Asbestos, betonrot/chlorideschade, soil contamination and oil tank are all answered **"niet bekend"** (honest, but unverified). Make the bid **subject to a satisfactory bouwkundige keuring** covering the structure, the rear-bedroom chimney-flue spot (a discoloured dry patch the seller flagged, now being inspected/repaired via the VvE), the electrical installation, and the internally-relocated washok/berging entrance.
4. **Insurance file is stale and the appartementenclausule is unconfirmed — LOW-MEDIUM.** The on-file polis runs 21-01-2022→01-01-2023 and shows **no appartementenclausule**; the seller ticked Q15b **"nee"**. **Request the current 2026 polisblad** and confirm (a) appartementenclausule, (b) foundations included in the rebuild value, (c) current insured sum/index.
5. **Energy label C — only partly "easily" improvable — LOW-MEDIUM.** Registered C (valid to 2035), but the cheap wins are largely **already done** (cavity walls, 2015 roof, double glazing, attic floor). Further gains — HR+++, mechanical ventilation/WTW, hybrid heat pump, solar — are **moderate** and the roof/façade are communal, needing VvE consent and money it has earmarked elsewhere. Heat demand stays elevated, natural ventilation only, 0% renewable. Treat as a small future project, not a quick flip to A.
6. **Splitsingstekening not in the set — LOW.** Q9l discloses the **washok/berging entrance was moved internally** (overloop → front bedroom) by previous owners. Disclosed as permit-free and VvE-consented, but **review the splitsingstekening**, confirm the layout matches, and name the correct berging in the koopakte.
7. **Dated bathroom (2003) — LOW.** ~23 yrs old; functional but budget for an eventual refresh.
8. **Thin VvE board — LOW (governance).** Down to **one board member** after two resignations (2026); technical committee being rebuilt (the nr-81 owner is joining it). Professionally administered so day-to-day is covered, but watch for a possible move to a **paid external board** that would lift costs. Decisions remain quorate and minuted.
9. **House-rules constraints — LOW.** Hard floors need a floating underlay to **Ico ≥ 10 dB** (NEN5077) — confirm the living-room laminate complies; window frames must be **RAL 9001** in plastic/aluminium/steel; airco/heat-pump outdoor units only at the rear with anthracite omkasting + kettingbeding; **no short-stay/B&B/commercial use**. €500 penalty regime. ## Price implication & bid recommendation

Asking €479k is already keen — **+1.7% over WOZ** and the **lowest €/m² of the three comps** — so the negotiating headroom is thinner than at nr 35 (which had started at €540k). A turnkey two-bed maisonette at this €/m² in the Stadshart can draw competition, so the strategy is to **bid decisively but condition tightly**, pricing in the looming VvE capex rather than expecting a deep discount.

- **Opening bid ~€465,000**, **target €470–475,000**, **walk-away ~€482,000** (a small premium is defensible if the survey and freehold check are clean and there is competition).
- Make the bid **subject to**: (1) satisfactory **bouwkundige keuring**; (2) **Kadaster/akte** confirming eigen grond and the rear-path easement; (3) **current VvE accounts + zero-arrears verklaring + the standleidingen/MJOP outlook**; (4) **current polisblad** confirming appartementenclausule and foundation cover; (5) **splitsingstekening** confirming the berging, named in the koopakte.
- **Walk away if** eigen grond cannot be confirmed, the standleidingen replacement turns into a near-term six-figure levy, or the survey surfaces foundation movement.

**Model note:** under the canonical rubric nr 81 lands ~7.5 — essentially level with nr 35 — held back *only* by tenure being "te verifieren"; **once eigen grond is documented, nr 81 becomes the stronger buy** (better label, lower €/m², healthier and larger VvE, storage that conveys).
