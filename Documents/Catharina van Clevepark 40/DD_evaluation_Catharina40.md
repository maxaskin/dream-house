# Due-diligence evaluation — Catharina van Clevepark 40, 1181 AT Amstelveen

**Prepared:** 2026-06-02 · Asking €485,000 · WOZ €446,000 (2025) · Built 1951 · VvE "Het Kasteel van Cleve" (Catharina van Clevepark 30–40)

Based on the seller's document set: NVM seller's questionnaire (Deel B), VvE 2026 minutes, VvE 2025 balance sheet & 2026 budget, MJOP 2020–2030, energy label, transfer deed (anonymised), floor plan, and broker's general terms.

---

## Verdict in one line

**Conditionally worth pursuing — but only at a renovation-adjusted price (~€390–405k) and with five hard conditions met.** Nothing in the documents is a deal-killer, but three items materially raise risk versus what we knew before: an active leak, a dangerously thin VvE reserve, and a pest disclosure that contradicts what Max saw on site.

---

## Advantages confirmed by the documents

1. **Freehold land (eigen grond).** The transfer deed describes an appartementsrecht on own land (total plot 14 a 08 ca); no ground lease (erfpacht). This removes a major Amstelveen risk and is a real plus.
2. **Only one, benign easement.** The deed records a single erfdienstbaarheid — a 1950 utility right for the Province of Noord-Holland to run electricity works across plot H-6160. Standard utility servitude, low impact. No onerous kettingbeding found.
3. **Usable area resolved in our favour.** The energy label states a gebruiksoppervlakte of **82.1 m²**; Funda lists 85 m² woonoppervlak. The scary 98 m² from kadasterdata was a gross/BAG figure, **not** usable space. So true €/m² is ~€5,800 on ~83 m² — still reasonable.
4. **Shell is decent.** Energy label A (registered 23-08-2023, valid to 24-11-2032); roof renewed ~1990 (Schiphol sound-insulation) and fully insulated; plastic (kunststof) double-glazed window frames. Seller reports **no** damp/penetrating moisture, **no** foundation defects.
5. **Seller did no unpermitted work.** Questionnaire: no alterations/extensions carried out, none without permit.
6. **Small, hands-on VvE; low fee.** ~12 members, self-administered; €78/mo for this unit; building insurance actively managed (sum insured €2,886,749 for 2026); members voted **not** to raise contributions for 2026.
7. **Parking.** Communal parking lot behind the building (street parking needs a permit).

## Risks found (ranked)

1. **Pest disclosure conflicts with the viewing — HIGH.** The questionnaire answers "**no** nuisance from vermin (mice/rats)" and "no woodworm/rot." Max saw steel wool packed into corner holes and a waste container directly in front. Either it was resolved historically or it is being under-disclosed. Because the questionnaire is a formal disclosure (mededelingsplicht), get a written clarification and physically inspect all entry points before committing.
2. **Active leak: bathroom → kitchen — MEDIUM-HIGH.** The questionnaire discloses "leakage from the bathroom into the kitchen" (consistent with the maisonette layout: bathroom upstairs, kitchen below). Source, extent and any hidden water damage/mould must be established. Folds into the gut budget but could signal a waterproofing/plumbing failure.
3. **VvE reserve dangerously low — MEDIUM-HIGH.** Reserve was **negative** end-2024 (−€2,632) after a ~€34k garage-street repaving, partly funded by **member loans** (some still outstanding). End-2025 reserve only ~€1,000–1,500. The statutory norm (0.5% of rebuild value) would be ~€11,777/yr; they add only ~€3,400. The 2026 minutes agree to **replace the roof ridge (nok) + add under-ridge sheeting** to stop leakage — agreed but effectively unfunded. A special assessment (suppletie) is likely, and as a **21/170 (~12%) share** owner you'd carry ~12% of any communal bill.
4. **Disclosed cracks/damage in the unit — MEDIUM.** Questionnaire: "(hidden) cracks/damage present: yes." Obtain the open-text detail; a bouwkundige keuring is essential.
5. **Asbestos unknown — MEDIUM.** 1951 build; seller "not known." A mandatory asbestos inventory (asbestinventarisatie) is needed before any gut; removal adds cost.
6. **Storage/garage/parking may not match the deed — MEDIUM.** Questionnaire Q1m = "**no**": the storage/garage/parking in use does **not** correspond to the splitsingsakte/tekening. Risk that a garage or parking spot you assume conveys does not legally transfer. The splitsingsakte + splitsingstekening were **not** in this set — request and review.
7. **Floor-plan provenance — MEDIUM (data hygiene).** The supplied plan file is named **"Oostzijde 92 A"** — a different address. The layout (2-floor maisonette, ~4.97 × 8.54 m, ~83 m²) is plausible for this unit, but it must be confirmed to depict CvC 40; do not rely on it for the NEN2580 area.
8. **Heritage / permits — LOW-MEDIUM.** Beschermd stadsgezicht and "beeldbepalend/karakteristiek" object confirmed; **not** a monument. Interior gut is permit-free, but any street-facing change (front-roof solar, facade, kozijnen) needs a permit. Confirm the existing kunststof kozijnen were permitted.
9. **Energy nuance — LOW.** Label A, but gas-heated, **0% renewable**, ~150.5 kWh/m²/yr fossil use. Greener upgrades (heat pump/solar) are future spend, and front-roof solar needs a heritage permit.
10. **Boundary encroachment — LOW.** Neighbours (Iepenrodelaan 2 / Eikenrodelaan) garden onto the VvE driveway; the VvE is formalising a written agreement to prevent adverse possession (verjaring). Communal, minor.

## Documents still to request

- **Splitsingsakte + splitsingstekening** (critical — settles the berging/garage/parking question and the exact share)
- **NEN2580 measurement report** (confirm 82–85 m²)
- **WOZ-beschikking** (official 2025 decision)
- **Building insurance policy** (copy; confirm sum insured & that garage uses are covered)
- **Open-text answers** in the questionnaire for the disclosed cracks and the bathroom→kitchen leak
- **Written confirmation** on the pest history and on the kunststof-kozijnen permit

## Price implication

The documents don't change the fair-value range (€390–420k as-is) but push toward the **lower half (~€390–405k)**: the leak, the disclosed cracks and the thin reserve / likely roof suppletie are all value-eroding. Recommendation: a conditional offer around **€400,000**, walk-away **€440,000**, explicitly subject to:

1. satisfactory bouwkundige keuring,
2. asbestos inventory,
3. splitsingsakte review confirming the parking/garage that conveys,
4. VvE confirming the roof-ridge cost and disclosing any planned suppletie,
5. written clarification of the pest history.

**Walk away if** the seller won't put the pest position in writing, or if the splitsingsakte shows the expected parking/garage does not transfer.
