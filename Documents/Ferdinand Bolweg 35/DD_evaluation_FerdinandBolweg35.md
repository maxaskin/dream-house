Based on the seller's document set (18 items): NVM questionnaire 2023, Lijst van zaken (13-05-2026), brochure, NEN2580 meetrapport (06-02-2026), energy label D (reg. 585116489, 18-02-2026), funderingsattest Fase 0 (30-04-2026), splitsingsakte 18-12-1981 + modelreglement 1973, akte van levering 02-01-2023, Kadaster eigendomsinformatie + kaart (04-02-2026), MJOP 2021, VvE besluit/notulen 13-03-2026, VvE bank statements + stand per 23-03-2026 (€8,290.63), opstalpolis Centraal Beheer + betaalbewijs 2026, VvE agenda 2022.

## Verdict in one line

**Worth pursuing — the cleanest condition file we've reviewed (certified 89 m², freehold, zero defect disclosures, foundation quickscan "low risk"), but the VvE is the weak spot: dormant for years, contributions only restarted 1-1-2026 via a round-robin decision rushed through for a previous buyer's financing, ~€8.3k reserve, and the 2022 maintenance plan possibly never executed.** Offer below asking; make the VvE paperwork a hard condition.

## Advantages confirmed by the documents

1. **Freehold (eigen grond), clean title.** Splitsingsakte (1981) + Kadaster: appartementsrecht H 11759 A-3 on own land, plot 2a 89ca; no erfpacht, no publiekrechtelijke beperkingen, no anti-speculatie/zelfbewoningsplicht, no monument or beschermd stadsgezicht (unlike Catharina), no disputes, no WOZ objection, not rented.
2. **Area certified.** NEN2580 meetrapport (Van Roemburg, type A, on-site 06-02-2026): **GO wonen 89 m²** (44.02 + 44.85), BVO 107 m², balcony 2 m². €485k / 89 m² = **€5,449/m²** — below Catharina's €5,706 and essentially **at WOZ** (€482k, +0.6%).
3. **Largest stake in the VvE.** 32/100 share and **32 of 100 votes** (33/37 have 18 each, 39 has 32). With one ally you reach 50–64% — real influence over insulation/solar/maintenance decisions.
4. **Zero defect disclosures.** Questionnaire: no damp, no leaks (roof/sanitary/elsewhere), no mould, no cracks, no floor/foundation defects, crawl space dry, no pests/woodworm, no asbestos ("nee", not "niet bekend"), no lead pipes, glazing without condensation. Far cleaner than Catharina 40 (leak, cracks, pest conflict).
5. **Foundation proactively screened.** Fase 0 funderingsattest (Perfectkeur, 30-04-2026): **"Geen actie / laag risico"** — no cracks, tilt nihil (lintvoeg/lood), no foundation-related symptoms. (Caveats at risk 3 below.)
6. **Recent renovation, documented.** Bathroom + both toilets 2023 (3rd bedroom converted to bathroom — Pepping loodgieter + Compier aannemer), kitchen 2019, appliances 2020, PVC herringbone floor 2023, carpets 2023; CV boiler 2013, serviced 2026 (Wagenaar). Lijst van zaken: floors, kitchen appliances, CV all stay; nothing leased.
7. **Insurance in order.** Centraal Beheer VvE Meerkeuzepolis: rebuild value €1,002,400 **including foundations** (GVU11) and underinsurance guarantee (GVU12), appartementenclausule confirmed, 2026 premium €757.31 paid (bank proof 02-01-2026).
8. **Service charge now legally correct.** From 1-1-2026: 0.5% of indexed rebuild value (€1,067,580) + insurance = €6,095.21/yr, split by votes → nr 35 pays 32% = €1,950.47/yr = **€162.54/mo** — matches the brochure exactly.
9. **Negotiation position.** Brochure asking was **€540,000**; now €485,000 (−10%). Seller bought 02-01-2023 for **€410,000** (deed + Kadaster). The 13-03-2026 VvE decision references an earlier "aspirant koper" whose financing needed the paperwork "this weekend" — that deal apparently fell through. Motivated seller, known cost basis.
10. **Location fits the brief.** Stadshart; 6 min bike to Emmakade 33 (ranked #3 overall, 7.0, in our model). Parking by permit (€40/yr; 2nd permit €80; visitor permit available).

## Risks found (ranked)

1. **VvE dormant for years; only just reactivated — HIGH.** The 13-03-2026 minutes state the VvE became "slapend" again in 2022, **everyone stopped paying service charges** at various points, no legally valid meetings were held (digital only; Q14a: not even annual), there is no professional administrator and the recorded bestuurder is not a VvE member (Q13). Contributions at the statutory level only start 1-1-2026, with a true-up to 1-10-2023 still being settled (nr 33 owed €220.30). The reactivation was done as a round-robin written decision expressly "for the financing of the prospective buyer." Lenders scrutinize exactly this; our own financing may face the same questions. **Get the fully signed decision + proof nr 35 has zero arrears (notarial VvE-verklaring will catch this at transfer).**
2. **2022 MJOP works possibly never executed — MEDIUM-HIGH.** MJOP (Sept 2021) planned **~€27,800** of 2022 works: balcony concrete repair, dakkapel repair, exterior painting (dakkapellen condition 4, balcony ceiling paint condition 5), gutters, boeiboorden, standpipe inspection. The VvE went dormant in 2022 — if these works weren't done they are now 4 years overdue and cost ~€31k indexed. **Nr 35's 32% share ≈ €10k exposure.** Reserve is only **€8,290.63** (23-03-2026) for the whole building. Next scheduled big spend: a roof package around 2034 (15-yr daken total €36,058, most items starting 2034). A suppletie is plausible despite Q16e answering "no".
3. **Wooden-pile foundation, area risk "ernstig" — MEDIUM.** Attest: type "paal hout" (inspector's presumption, not verified), **Fundermaps area risk indication: ernstig**, trees nearby flagged as possibly affecting the foundation. Object-level result is clean today, but Fase 0 is a visual quickscan, not a Fase 1/2 investigation; wooden piles of 1955 in Amstelveen carry long-term repair tail-risk, communal at 32% share. Positive offset: foundations are inside the insured rebuild value.
4. **Berging does not match the deed — MEDIUM.** Q1m = "**no**": the storage in use does not correspond to the splitsingsakte/tekening. The 1981 akte gives nr 35 a **box on the ground floor**; the brochure sells an "eigen stenen berging aan de achterzijde"; the NEN2580 report records **0 m² external storage**. Same failure mode as Catharina: a box you assume conveys may legally belong to a neighbour. The **splitsingstekening is missing** from the set — request it and name the correct box in the koopakte.
5. **Energy label D, with the expensive parts communal — MEDIUM.** Registered label (18-02-2026): 277.9 kWh/m² fossil, heat demand 177 vs standard 57, **0% renewable**, walls RC 0.19–0.35, roof RC 0.22–0.35 (both effectively uninsulated), windows Uw 1.80 (ordinary double glazing), natural ventilation only, high summer-overheating risk. Roof/facade/kozijnen are VvE elements — improvement needs VvE consent and money the VvE doesn't have. Upside: as 32%-vote owner in a 4-member VvE, consent is achievable; private quick wins (hybrid heat pump — CV is privé per the akte, internal roof insulation with consent, HR++ in existing frames) could plausibly reach label B, budget €20–40k. Under our model "easily improvable" is only **partly** true here.
6. **2023 conversion: contractor shares the seller's surname — LOW-MEDIUM.** Bathroom/toilet built upstairs by "Compier aannemer"; seller is R.M. Compier. Possibly family work. Q9m/n say no permit was needed and VvE consent wasn't lacking, and an interior conversion is normally permit-free — but get invoices, waterproofing details and any warranty, and written confirmation the VvE consented to plumbing changes through communal shafts.
7. **No smoke detectors (Q7o "nee") — LOW.** Legally required on every floor since 1 July 2022. Trivial cost, but it signals the questionnaire was filled honestly — and the unit isn't fully compliant on handover.
8. **Electrical installation age unknown — LOW.** Q7k "niet bekend" whether wiring was renewed (1955 building), though the brochure claims a "moderne meterkast". Ask what was renewed; have the bouwkundige check the installation.
9. **Path easement — LOW (benign).** 1981 akte: right of way on the rear path serving the bergingen; owners contribute 1/19 each to its maintenance, managed via VvE Ferdinand Bolweg 1–7. Minor recurring obligation; confirm it's dormant in practice.
10. **Data inconsistencies — LOW (hygiene).** MJOP says build year 1953 vs 1955 elsewhere; energy label floor area 91 m² vs NEN 89 m²; questionnaire reserve "€9,000" vs bank €8,290.63; seller's bank transfer to the VvE on 03-02-2026 was €130.70 where a quarter is €487.62 — ask what that payment was.

## Price implication

Asking €485k is fair against WOZ (€482k) and our model (price score 9; ranked #3 at 7.0) — but the brochure's €540k start, a listing evidently prepared in early Feb 2026 (Kadaster extract 04-02, measurement 06-02, label 18-02), a likely fallen-through March buyer, and the seller's €410k cost basis all point to negotiating room, while risks 1–2 justify a documented discount (~€10k deferred-maintenance exposure + VvE credibility). Recommendation: **conditional opening offer ~€455,000, target ≤€470,000, walk-away €480,000**, explicitly subject to:

1. satisfactory bouwkundige keuring (incl. electrical installation and 2023 plumbing),
2. fully signed 13-03-2026 VvE decision + VvE-verklaring of zero arrears and no planned suppletie,
3. written status of the 2022 MJOP works (done with invoices, or price abatement/escrow),
4. splitsingstekening review confirming which box conveys, named in the koopakte,
5. invoices/warranty for the 2023 conversion + VvE consent in writing.

**Walk away if** the VvE decision can't be produced fully signed (financing risk for us, resale risk later), if the berging proves not to convey, or if anything surfaces suggesting foundation movement (then demand a Fase 1 investigation before proceeding).
